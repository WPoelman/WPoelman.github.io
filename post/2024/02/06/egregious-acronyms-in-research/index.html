Egregious Acronyms in Research

Wessel Poelman

2024/02/06

This is a collection of some ‘interesting’ acronyms used in research that I’ve encountered or that people have send to me. Most of these papers I’ve not read (and will probably never), it’s just about the acronyms (and backronyms). Feel free to send me some samples, I’ll decide if they are egregious enough to make the list (with credits of course).

A next step would be automatic collection of these, but that removes some of the fun of encountering them in the wild. I’m leaving that be for now.

Last updated: Feb 06, 2024.

  • CAPIVARA: Cost-Efficient Approach for Improving Multilingual CLIP Performance on Low-Resource Languages
  • CATfOOD: Counterfactual Augmented Training for Improving Out-of-Domain Performance and Calibration (Esther)
  • EPIC: Multi-Perspective Annotation of a Corpus of Irony (Esther)
  • GENOME: GenerativE Neuro-symbOlic visual reasoning by growing and reusing ModulEs (Miryam)
  • GRENADE: Graph-Centric Language Model for Self-Supervised Representation Learning on Text-Attributed Graphs
